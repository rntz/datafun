\Cref{fig:phi,fig:delta} define two mutually recursive static transformations:
the speed-up $\phi e$, which computes fixed points faster by using derivatives;
and $\delta e$, which computes the derivative of $\phi e$. To explain these
transformations, we must first explain what they do to types.

Following the approach of the incremental \fn-calculus, for each type $A$ we
define a type of changes $\D A$:

\begin{align*}
  \D\tunit &= \tunit
  &
  \D(A \x B) &= \D A \x \D B
  \\
  \D \iso A &= \tunit
  &
  \D(A + B) &= \D A + \D B
  \\
  \D\tseteq A &= \tseteq A
  &
  \D(A \to B) &= \iso A \to \D A \to \D B
\end{align*}
